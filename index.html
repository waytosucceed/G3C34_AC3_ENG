Past Perfect Tense

The past perfect tense helps us understand what happened first in a story about the past. It's like looking back at something that happened before another event in the past.

Rule: Subject + had + past participle + the rest of the sentence.

ðŸ‘‰ We use "had" for all subjects (I, you, he, she, it, we, they).
Example: "The class had started before Tom arrived."
  • Subject: The class
  • Had: used for the past perfect tense
  • Started: past participle of the verb "to start"

    • This sentence shows that the class started before Tom arrived. Both events are in the past, but the class started first.

    Simple Past Tense
    The simple past tense is used to talk about something that happened and was finished in the past.

    Rule: Subject + Verb in the past form (base form of the verb + ed/d for regular verbs or past tense form of the irregular verbs).

    For regular verbs, add "-ed" to the end of the verb (like "play" becomes "played"). For irregular verbs, the word changes in its own way (like "go" becomes "went").

    Examples:
    "I played with my toys yesterday."
    "She ate ice cream last night."
    Past Continuous Tense
    The past continuous tense is used to talk about something that was happening in the past for a period of time. It shows that the action was ongoing.

    Rule: Subject + was/were + present participle (verb+ing) + the rest of the sentence.

    We use "was" for I/he/she/it and "were" for you/we/they.

    Examples:
    "I was playing outside when it started to rain."
    "She was reading a book while her brother was drawing."
